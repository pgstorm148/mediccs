Tania S. Malik, J.D. is a Co-Founder and CEO of Virtual Medical Services, PC. She has spent the past 23 years dedicated to creating digital health companies to solve unmet needs in healthcare.


Tania’s digital health experience includes the development and growth of three telehealth companies that combined to provide an online consultation with a national network of board-certified physicians where the treatment plan included a prescription. Additionally, Tania started a telehealth company that focused on naturopathic and integration medicine consultations which she implemented with a national payor.


However, Tania’s true passions of honoring military veterans and using technology to solve healthcare problems came together when she was inspired to start COPE Today, a tele-mental health solution. COPE Today was started in response to the Iraq and Afghanistan Wars when she felt our country was not prepared for the severity of mental health conditions the soldiers were experiencing when they returned home. COPE Today’s first implementation was with the North Carolina National Guard where she began to deepen her understanding, gratitude. and respect for those that serve. She sold COPE Today to Mindcare Solutions Group, Inc., which continues to provide mental health treatment to all populations in need.


In conjunction with COPE Today, she started COPE Foundation (a/k/a Military Veteran Resource Coalition) which provided service members and veterans with emergency funding to help with needs such as paying electric bills to keep power or rent to prevent homelessness.


Today, she is President and one of the founding Board of Directors for In-Kind Friends, a non- profit that helps other non-profits amplify their community impact. She is a leader in the American Telemedicine Association, being the immediate Past Chair of the Telemental Health Special Interest Group. In 2020, she was awarded the ATA Woman of the Year and in 2021 she was elected to the ATA College of Fellows.


She earned a bachelor’s degrees from the University of North Carolina in Chapel Hill and a Juris Doctorate from Georgia State University. She is licensed to practice law in both North Carolina and Georgia. She serves on two Strategic Advisor Boards-Innovatel Telepsychiatry, just acquired by Quartet, and Omnysense, a remote patient monitoring company.


Tania has a passion for helping others and considers it an honor to support the veteran community and is grateful for their service.

Colonel Mark E. McDaniel is the State Air Surgeon, West Virginia Air National Guard, West Virginia Joint Force Headquarters, McLaughlin Air National Guard Base, West Virginia. He is the senior medical adviser to the Assistant Adjutant General and Commander of the West Virginia Air National Guard. As the State Air Surgeon he makes recommendations, provides input and proposes solution sets affecting over 2,150 members and two flying units


Colonel McDaniel joined the Air Force in 1996 as a Captain while in his Family Medicine residency program in Clarksburg, W.Va. He entered active duty in 1998 and was stationed at Keesler Air Force base in Biloxi, Miss. in the Family Medicine clinic. In 2000 he attended the Aerospace Medicine Primary Course to become a Flight Surgeon and then transferred to Royal Air Force, Upwood, United Kingdom and then subsequently to RAF Mildenhall, UK where he was a Flight Surgeon and Medical Flight Commander for the 351st Air Refueling Squadron. During his career he has deployed to various austere locations to include: Oman, Kuwait, Afghanistan, Iraq, Djibouti, Peru, Mozambique, Grenada and Turkey. Also, he has served at Geilenkirchen, NATO Air Base and has served as the lead medical officer on two separate deployments to Germany as part of a Contingency Air Staging Facility. Colonel McDaniel, served as a NASA/DoD Transatlantic Atlantic Landing Flight Surgeon for the Space Shuttle Program. After his active duty commitment, he joined the Air Force Reserves and served with the 53rd Weather Reconnaissance Squadron in Biloxi, Miss. and then as an Air Force Reserves Individual Medical Augmentee. He later joined the Air National Guard at the 130th Medical Group, McLaughlin Air National Guard Base in Charleston, W,Va., where he has served as the Chief of Clinical Medicine and the Chief of Aerospace Medicine.


In his civilian capacity, he is the Medical Director at St Mary’s Urgent Care in Huntington, W.Va. and a managing partner of Mountain State Recovery Center LLC and Versacare Medical LLC, in Charleston, W.Va. He is also employed by Royal Caribbean Cruise Lines and Norwegian Cruise Lines where he is employed as a Senior Ship Physician.
